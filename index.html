✨ Aurora Borealis

The northern lights · nature's most mesmerising light show

The Northern Lights, or Aurora Borealis, are one of the most spectacular natural phenomena on Earth. They appear as shimmering curtains, arcs, and spirals of green, pink, violet, and red light dancing across the polar sky. This breathtaking display is caused by charged particles from the Sun colliding with gas atoms in Earth's upper atmosphere. The colours depend on the type of gas and the altitude: oxygen gives green and red, while nitrogen produces blue and purple hues. The aurora is most visible in high‑latitude regions such as Norway, Sweden, Finland, Iceland, Canada, and Alaska.

For centuries, indigenous cultures have woven legends around the aurora. In Finnish mythology, the lights were thought to be caused by a magical fox sweeping snow into the sky with its tail. The scientific explanation is equally fascinating: solar wind carries particles that are guided by Earth's magnetic field toward the poles, where they excite oxygen and nitrogen molecules, releasing photons of light. The intensity of the aurora varies with the solar cycle; during solar maximum, the displays are especially vivid and can even be seen further south. Many travellers call witnessing the aurora a "bucket‑list" experience — and once you see it, you never forget the feeling of standing under a celestial symphony of light.

✦ best places to see the aurora

  • Tromsø, Norway — "the gateway to the Arctic" with frequent aurora displays.
  • Abisko, Sweden — known for its clear skies and the famous "blue hole".
  • Rovaniemi, Finland — Santa's hometown, also a prime aurora spot.
  • Reykjavík, Iceland — accessible and surrounded by dark volcanic landscapes.
  • Yellowknife, Canada — one of the best aurora viewing locations in North America.
  • Fairbanks, Alaska — located directly under the auroral oval.

Whether you are an avid photographer, a nature lover, or simply curious, the aurora offers a magical encounter with the cosmos. The experience of watching the sky pulse and ripple in silence is profoundly moving — a reminder of the beauty and power of our planet. With a little patience and a dark, clear sky, the Northern Lights might just put on a show you will carry with you forever.
